## Extended Bio (200 words)

Matt Guerra is a Texas-based visual artist whose practice explores the intersections of identity, mental health, and human vulnerability. Working across photography, video art, mixed media, and alternative processes including cyanotype printing, Guerra creates deeply personal work that transforms lived experience into powerful visual advocacy.

His artistic journey began with medium format film photography and has evolved to encompass scratched and burned negatives, handmade artist books, video installations, and portrait series. Central to all his work is an unflinching examination of his own experiences with addiction, depression, bipolar disorder, and the ongoing search for identity.

Guerra's practice operates on the belief that art can be both deeply personal and profoundly connective. By sharing his struggles openly, he aims to destigmatize conversations around mental health, validate others' experiences, and advocate for better mental health care—particularly for those battling addiction.

His work has been recognized for its raw emotional honesty and its ability to transform darkness into dialogue. Whether through the ethereal blue tones of cyanotype portraits or the visceral imagery of self-destruction and recovery, Guerra's art invites viewers into difficult conversations while offering hope and the reminder that connection can save lives.
